In certain packaging areas, surfaces become greasy or sticky very quickly, sometimes even with multiple daily cleanings. This phenomenon, far from trivial, reveals a progressive accumulation of organic micro-deposits that conventional protocols fail to eliminate effectively.

Why surfaces become sticky so fast

Organic micro-deposits accumulate progressively during production operations. When production rates are high, residues quickly attach to contact surfaces:

  • Stainless steel surfaces: Despite their apparent ease of maintenance, stainless steel surfaces retain invisible protein films that become progressively adherent.
  • Plastics: Plastic materials in packaging zones absorb greasy residues and become increasingly difficult to clean over time.
  • Handles and controls: Direct handling zones continuously accumulate human contact and food residues.
  • Product contact zones: Each product pass leaves a microscopic organic trace that adds up over the hours.

The consequences on production

These accumulations generate concrete problems that impact both quality and operational efficiency:

  • Discomfort for operators working on sticky surfaces
  • Increased risk of cross-contamination between production batches
  • End-of-day cleaning made more difficult and longer
  • Progressive deterioration of quality control results

Key takeaway

In packaging areas, maintenance frequency sometimes becomes more important than the amount of product used. Regular quick intermediate cleaning prevents organic accumulation far more effectively than a single major end-of-day cleaning.
